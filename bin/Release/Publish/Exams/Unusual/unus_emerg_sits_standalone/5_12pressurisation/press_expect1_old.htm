PRESSURISATION PROBLEMS: EXPECT

(To be designed)

Pressurisation problems can seriously impair the consciousness of the pilot. (from CD - check/expand?))

The pilot will have much to do on board and so will be limited in his or her actions. ATC may therefore be informed very late and there may be no emergency squawk.

The ACFT will stop climb / Request for immediate descent / Emergency descent without warning:
In order to reach altitudes where the human body can survive without bottled oxygen, the crew will perform a rapid descent to FL 150 - FL 100. The atmosphere becomes breathable at around 14000 ft and below. The rate of descent will be 7000 ft/min to 14000 ft/min.

!!!BY DOING THIS, THE AIRCRAFT WILL DESCEND THROUGH OTHER LEVELS AND SEPARATION TO OTHER AIRCRAFT MAY BE INFRINGED!!!

During descent, the pilot will turn 15º to 45º off track.

R/T will be poor due to noise, as the pilot is now communicating via a microphone in the oxygen mask.
